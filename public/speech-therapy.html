Interactive Speech Therapy

Practice clinical exercises to target specific speech disorders, deepen vocal pitch, or improve articulation agility.

Clinical Exercises

1

Stuttering & Stammering (Fluency)

For stuttering. Focuses on reducing tension when starting words.

Practice: **Easy Onset** (Start with a soft, warm breath before vocalizing) and **Light Contact** (touch lips/tongue gently).
2

Apraxia of Speech (Motor Planning)

For coordinate brain-to-mouth muscle planning. Focuses on syllable transitions.

Practice: **Syllable Pacing** (Tap your finger for every syllable, maintaining an equal, slow rhythm).
3

Dysarthria (Muscle Weakness)

For slurred speech due to muscle weakness. Focuses on strength and clarity.

Practice: **Over-Articulation** (Exaggerate consonant sounds like 'b', 'p', 't', 'k' and project from the diaphragm).
4

Lisp (Articulation Disorder)

For interdental protrusion (tongue between teeth on 'S'/'Z' sounds).

Practice: **Closed-Teeth Placement** (Keep your teeth closed and place your tongue tip against the roof of your mouth behind your front teeth).
5

Voice Deepening & Chest Resonance

To safely lower vocal pitch, develop a richer timbre, and prevent high nasal strain.

Practice: **Yawn-Sigh Technique** (Simulate a yawn to lower your larynx, relax your throat, and project sound from your chest).
6

Tongue Twisters (Articulatory Agility)

For speed control, muscular coordination, and rapid articulation agility.

Practice: **Incremental Pacing** (Start very slowly, focusing on perfect clarity, then gradually increase speed).
7

Aphasia (Language Retrieval)

For word-finding and verbal expression issues (typically post-stroke).

Practice: **Sentence Completion** (Speak the prompt and complete the missing word at the end out loud).
8

Dysphonia (Vocal Strain & Pitch)

For hoarse, strained, or weak vocal quality. Focuses on vocal hygiene.

Practice: **Resonant Voice Humming** (Hum gently focusing the vibration in your lips and nose, then slide your pitch up and down).
9

Cluttering (Rapid / Irregular Speech)

For abnormally rapid, jerky, or disorganized speech pacing.

Practice: **Punctuation Pausing** (Stop and take a full breath at every comma and period. Do not rush the syllables).

Natural Vocal Care & Cures

Nourish and protect your vocal folds naturally to prevent strain, maintain elasticity, and support a deeper, resonant speaking register.

Warm Ginger & Honey

Acts as an organic anti-inflammatory and demulcent. Soothes vocal fold tissues and reduces swelling or strain after prolonged speaking.

Slippery Elm Tea

Contains high levels of mucilage, which coats the throat lining, lubricating the vocal folds and preventing vocal raspiness or cracking.

Steam Inhalation

Inhaling warm steam directly hydrates the vocal folds. Thin mucosal layers allow the vocal folds to vibrate more freely, facilitating a deeper tone.

Hydration (Lukewarm)

Drink 2-3 liters of room-temperature water. Avoid ice-cold water (which constricts throat muscles) and caffeine (which dries out the vocal cords).

*Clinical Disclaimer*: Natural vocal remedies provide supportive care. Chronic hoarseness or sudden loss of voice lasting more than two weeks should be evaluated by an Otolaryngologist (ENT).
